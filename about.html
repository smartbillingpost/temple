About Annapurna Sameta Kashi Vishweshwara Swamy Temple

A sacred spiritual center at Narasingapadu dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Annapurna.

About the Temple

The Annapurna Sameta Kashi Vishweshwara Swamy Temple at Narasingapadu is a sacred shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Annapurna. Devotees believe that worship here brings peace, prosperity, and spiritual growth.

The temple reflects the sacred traditions of Kashi and serves as a center of devotion, annadanam, and community welfare.

Temple History

According to local traditions, the temple has been a place of worship for several decades. It was established with the devotion of local villagers who wished to bring the divine energy of Kashi to their region.

Lord Kashi Vishweshwara and Goddess Annapurna are worshipped together, symbolizing wisdom and nourishment. Many devotees have experienced divine blessings and miracles through sincere prayers.

The temple continues to grow with the support of devotees and the temple committee, preserving spiritual traditions and Vedic rituals.

Festivals & Rituals

The temple celebrates several sacred festivals and monthly rituals with great devotion. These spiritual observances bring the village community together to worship Lord Shiva and Goddess Annapurna through poojas, abhishekams, and traditional ceremonies. Devotees gather throughout the year to participate in these divine occasions seeking blessings, prosperity, and spiritual peace.

Masa Sivaratri

Monthly Shivaratri pooja with abhishekam and special prayers to Lord Shiva.

Masa Pournima

Sacred full moon day rituals performed with devotional offerings.

Masa Arudra Nakshatram

Special pooja conducted on Arudra Nakshatram dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Masa Nandi Pooja

Monthly pooja offered to Nandi, the sacred vehicle of Lord Shiva.

Maha Sivaratri

One of the grandest celebrations with night-long prayers, abhishekam, and bhajans.

Dasara Navaratrulu

Nine sacred days celebrating divine energy with special poojas and rituals.

Karthikamasam

Holy month dedicated to Lord Shiva with deepam lighting and devotional worship.

Village Day

A special annual celebration where the entire village gathers for prayers and community worship.
